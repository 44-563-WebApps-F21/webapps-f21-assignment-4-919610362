A Woman of no Importance

ACT III. _The Hall at Hunstanton Chase_. [LORD ILLINGWORTH _and_ GERALD, R.C. LORD ILLINGWORTH _lolling on a sofa_. GERALD _in a chair_.]

GERALD.

[_Laughing_.]

I might be able to learn how to tie a tie, Lord Illingworth, but I should never be able to talk as you do. I don’t know how to talk.

LORD ILLINGWORTH.

Oh! talk to every woman as if you loved her, and to every man as if he bored you, and at the end of your first season you will have the reputation of possessing the most perfect social tact.

GERALD.

But it is very difficult to get into society isn’t it?

LORD ILLINGWORTH.

To get into the best society, nowadays, one has either to feed people, amuse people, or shock people—that is all!

GERALD.

I suppose society is wonderfully delightful!
